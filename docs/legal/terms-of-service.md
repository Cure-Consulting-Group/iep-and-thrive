## 2. Program Description

IEP & Thrive provides a private, supplemental summer education program for students with Individualized Education Programs (IEPs), 504 Plans, or documented learning differences. The program operates during summer months and offers small-group instruction in reading, math, and academic skills.

**Important Clarification:**
- IEP & Thrive is a **private educational enrichment** and **tutoring** program
- We are **NOT** a school, school district, or agency of the New York State Education Department
- We do **NOT** provide Extended School Year (ESY) services, Related Services (OT, PT, Speech), or any mandated services under IDEA
- We do **NOT** modify, replace, or supersede a student's IEP or 504 Plan
- Our progress reports are for **informational purposes only** and do not constitute official educational records
- Enrollment in our program does not guarantee any specific academic outcome

## 3. Eligibility

The Program is designed for students in grades K–6 (approximately ages 5–12) with:
- Active IEPs
- 504 Plans
- Documented learning differences (e.g., dyslexia, dyscalculia, ADHD)
- Other learning needs as discussed during discovery call

Eligibility is determined at the sole discretion of IEP & Thrive based on a discovery call and review of the student's learning profile.

## 4. Enrollment & Payment

### 4.1 Enrollment Process
1. Complete a discovery call with the Program Director
2. Receive enrollment approval
3. Submit a 25% non-refundable deposit via the website to secure your spot
4. Complete the Parent Intake Form (including IEP/504 upload, medical information, emergency contacts)
5. Pay the remaining balance by the posted deadline

### 4.2 Payment Terms
- **Deposit:** 25% of tuition, due at enrollment. Non-refundable except as described in Section 5.
- **Balance:** Remaining 75% due by June 23, 2026
- **Accepted methods:** Credit/debit card via Stripe
- **Late payment:** Spots may be forfeited if balance is not received by the deadline
- **Returned payments:** A $35 fee applies to any returned/declined payments

### 4.3 What's Included
- 6 weeks of small-group instruction (up to 6 students)
- Pre-program IEP review and goal alignment
- 6 weekly progress reports (emailed as PDF)
- Final comprehensive CSE-ready report
- Daily parent debrief at pickup
- All program materials and supplies

### 4.4 What's NOT Included
- Related services (OT, PT, Speech, Counseling)
- Transportation
- Snacks/meals (families provide daily)
- 1:1 aide services
- Pre- or after-care

## 6. Program Expectations

### 6.1 Attendance
- Students are expected to attend all scheduled sessions
- 3 or more unexcused absences may result in dismissal from the program without refund
- Parents must notify us of absences by 8:00 AM on the day of absence

### 6.2 Behavior
- Students are expected to participate respectfully in all activities
- IEP & Thrive implements positive behavioral supports and trauma-informed practices
- In the event of persistent disruptive behavior that compromises the safety or learning of other students, IEP & Thrive reserves the right to dismiss a student from the program. In such cases, a pro-rata credit may be issued at our sole discretion.

### 6.3 Drop-Off & Pick-Up
- Program runs 9:00 AM – 1:00 PM, Monday through Thursday
- Drop-off begins at 8:45 AM
- Pick-up must occur by 1:15 PM
- Students will only be released to authorized individuals listed on the enrollment form
- Repeated late pick-up may result in a $25/occurrence late fee

### 6.4 Health & Safety
- Parents must disclose all relevant medical information, allergies, and medications
- If a student becomes ill during the program, parents will be contacted for immediate pick-up
- Students with fever (100.4°F+), vomiting, or contagious illness must remain home for 24 hours symptom-free before returning
