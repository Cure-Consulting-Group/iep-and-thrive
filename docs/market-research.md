---

## 1. Executive Summary

Long Island has **69,125 students with disabilities** across Nassau and Suffolk counties — a 10% increase over 5 years — yet private, IEP-aligned summer programming remains critically undersupplied. Most families are either denied Extended School Year (ESY) services or receive generic camp programs unrelated to their child's IEP goals. IEP & Thrive fills this gap with a credentialed, small-group intensive priced competitively at $3,500–$4,000 — below Winston Prep ($4,500/4 wks) and dramatically below full-inclusion camps ($9,675/8 wks). The confluence of rising SPED populations, widespread ESY denial, and Orton-Gillingham hourly rates of $100–$200/hr on Long Island creates a clear market opportunity. **Recommendation: Enter.**

### Value Comparison

IEP & Thrive delivers **96 hours of direct instruction** over 6 weeks. At $4,000 (Full Academic), that's:
- **$41.67/hour** of credentialed SPED instruction
- **vs. $100–$200/hr** for equivalent private OG tutoring
- **58–79% savings** compared to hiring a private specialist

### Pricing Recommendation

Current pricing ($3,500 Reading/Math, $4,000 Full Academic) is **well-positioned:**
- Below Winston Prep on a per-week basis ($583–$667/wk vs. $1,125/wk)
- Dramatically below JCC programs ($5,800–$9,675)
- Significant savings vs. private tutoring
- 25% deposit model reduces enrollment friction

> [!TIP]
> **Consider an early-bird discount** (5-10% off for enrollments before March 31) to front-load demand and secure cohort viability. Also consider a sibling discount (10-15%) to increase household conversion.
