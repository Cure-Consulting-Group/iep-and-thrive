# Week 3 — "Building Blocks"
## Theme: How Things Are Built — Words, Numbers, Structures

**Dates**: July 21–25, 2026
**Focus**: Vowel teams (Part 2), r-controlled vowels, narrative writing, multiplication and division, syllable type consolidation

---

## Weekly Learning Objectives

### Reading & Phonics (OG)
- Teach remaining vowel teams: oi/oy (coin, boy), ou/ow (house, cow), au/aw (sauce, draw)
- Note: ow is a "switch hitter" — can say /oh/ (snow) or /ow/ (cow); teach both and practice sorting
- Introduce r-controlled vowels: ar (car), or (fork), er/ir/ur (her, bird, burn — same sound, three spellings)
- Multisensory practice: arm tapping for syllable division, sky writing for new phonograms, phonogram card drill
- Cumulative review: closed, open (introduced briefly), VCe (reviewed), vowel team, r-controlled syllable types
- OG dictation includes mixed patterns from Weeks 1–3
- Red words for the week: thought, through, enough, laugh, bought, caught
- Decodable text practice with r-controlled and vowel team patterns

### Comprehension
- Teach story elements explicitly: character (who), setting (where/when), problem (what goes wrong), solution (how it is fixed)
- Practice sequencing events using transition words: first, next, then, after that, finally
- Introduce story map graphic organizer (character, setting, problem, events, solution)
- Compare elements across two texts read during the week (Venn diagram)
- Reading response journal: "Draw and write about the problem and solution in today's story"

### Writing
- Begin narrative writing unit: planning phase
- Monday: Brainstorm story ideas using a "story seed" list (small moments, personal experiences)
- Tuesday: Story planning with graphic organizer — choose character, setting, problem
- Wednesday: Write strong openings (hooks) — teach 3 types: action, dialogue, question
- Thursday: Introduce dialogue — punctuation rules (quotation marks, comma, new speaker = new line)
- Friday: Draft opening paragraph of narrative story (portfolio piece — drafting stage)
- Enrichment connection: stories can be set in a building, museum, or structure explored during the week

### Math
- Introduce division as sharing (partitive: 12 cookies shared among 3 friends) using concrete manipulatives
- Introduce division as grouping (measurement: how many groups of 4 in 20?) using counters and division mats
- Teach the relationship between multiplication and division as inverse operations
- Introduce fact families (e.g., 3 x 4 = 12, 4 x 3 = 12, 12 / 4 = 3, 12 / 3 = 4)
- Word problem schemas: equal groups (multiplication and division), comparison problems
- CRA progression: Monday–Tuesday concrete (counters on division mats), Wednesday representational (bar models, grouping diagrams), Thursday–Friday abstract (equations, fact families, word problems)
- Continue multiplication fact fluency practice (x0, x1, x2, x5, x10) in daily warm-up

### Executive Function & SEL
- Introduce planning as a strategy: "Before I start, I need to think about..." (applies to writing and STEM)
- Practice breaking a large task into smaller steps (connects to building/construction theme)
- Self-monitoring: add a "planning check" to the daily checklist — "Did I make a plan before I started?"
- SEL focus: teamwork and collaboration during building challenges — giving/receiving feedback constructively
- Introduce "growth mindset" language: "I can't do this YET" — connect to new challenging phonics patterns

## Differentiation Notes

### Flexible Grouping (Adjusted Based on Mid-Cycle Check #1)

**Phonics Groups**:
- Group A: Students still working on Week 2 vowel teams (ai/ay, ee/ea, oa/ow) — continue practicing those with daily drill; introduce r-controlled vowels (ar, or only — limit new patterns); heavy use of decodable texts at vowel team level
- Group B: Week 2 vowel teams solid — teach all Week 3 patterns (oi/oy, ou/ow, au/aw + ar, or, er/ir/ur); standard OG lesson cycle; practice sorting r-controlled patterns
- Group C: Quick acquisition of vowel teams — teach all Week 3 patterns with emphasis on encoding (spelling in dictation); add multisyllabic words with r-controlled vowels (garden, corner, perfect); begin syllable division preview with compound words

**Math Groups**:
- Group A: Focus on multiplication concepts — use division only in concrete sharing context; keep factors small (dividing by 2, 5, 10); continue building multiplication fact fluency
- Group B: Full multiplication and division progression; fact families with x2, x5, x10 families; standard word problem practice with bar models
- Group C: Accelerated fact family work; introduce x3, x4, x6 facts; two-step word problems involving both operations; challenge with "missing factor" problems (__ x 4 = 24)

**Comprehension**: All students use the story map graphic organizer. Differentiate by text complexity:
- Below-level readers: use teacher read-alouds for comprehension; student-read texts at instructional level for practice
- On-level readers: independently read grade-level narrative passages
- Text comparison (Venn diagram): pair students strategically so stronger readers can support during discussion

**Writing**: All students plan and begin drafting a narrative.
- Students who need more support: use the story map as a direct drafting guide; provide sentence starters for the opening
- Students ready for more: experiment with all three hook types; add dialogue to their opening paragraph
- All students: conference individually at least once during the week
