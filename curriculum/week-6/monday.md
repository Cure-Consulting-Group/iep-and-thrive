### Math Block (9:20–10:20)

**Objective**: Students will solve multi-step word problems using a strategy toolkit (draw a picture, make a table, work backward, number line, write an equation, look for a pattern). Second half: complete the Math Computation Post-Assessment (mirrors Week 1). (IEP Goal: Math Problem Solving — solve 1- and 2-step word problems with >=75% accuracy, showing work.)

**Materials**:
- Strategy toolkit reference poster (posted — with visual for each strategy)
- Multi-step word problem cards (12 problems — graded difficulty)
- Dry-erase boards + markers
- All manipulatives available at choice table (base-ten blocks, counters, fraction strips, play money, clocks, rulers, number lines)
- Math Computation Post-Assessment (mirrors Week 1 exactly):
  - Addition/subtraction fact fluency (2 min timed)
  - Multi-digit addition and subtraction (untimed)
  - Multiplication/division facts (2 min timed)
  - Place value questions
  - Fraction problems
- Pencils + scratch paper

#### Strategy Toolkit Review (15 min)

1. **Review All Strategies (5 min)**:
   - Point to each strategy on the poster:
     - **Draw a picture**: Sketch the problem. Helpful for fractions, measurement, grouping.
     - **Make a table**: Organize information in rows and columns. Helpful for patterns and multi-step.
     - **Work backward**: Start with the answer and undo steps. Helpful for "how many left" or "what was the start."
     - **Number line**: Place numbers and jump. Helpful for addition, subtraction, fractions, elapsed time.
     - **Write an equation**: Translate words to symbols. Helpful when numbers are clear.
     - **Look for a pattern**: Find the rule. Helpful for repeated operations.
   - *"The most powerful math skill isn't knowing the answer — it's knowing WHICH STRATEGY to use."*

2. **Guided Practice — "Strategy Match" (10 min)**:
   - Read 4 word problems aloud. For each, students discuss which strategy would work best, then solve.

   **Problem 1**: *"Mia has 24 stickers. She wants to share them equally among 6 friends. How many stickers does each friend get?"*
   - Best strategy: Draw a picture (equal groups) or write an equation (24 / 6 = 4).

   **Problem 2**: *"A garden has 3 rows with 8 tomato plants in each row. Then 5 plants died. How many tomato plants are left?"*
   - Best strategy: Write an equation (3 x 8 = 24, then 24 - 5 = 19). Two steps!

   **Problem 3**: *"Jake had some baseball cards. He gave 12 to his friend and has 15 left. How many did he start with?"*
   - Best strategy: Work backward (15 + 12 = 27).

   **Problem 4**: *"On Monday, Sara read 1/4 of her book. On Tuesday, she read 2/4 more. How much has she read in all? How much is left?"*
   - Best strategy: Number line or fraction strips. 1/4 + 2/4 = 3/4 read. 4/4 - 3/4 = 1/4 left.

   - After each: *"Did anyone use a DIFFERENT strategy? That's great — there's often more than one way!"*

#### Math Computation Post-Assessment (25 min)

1. **Administration (20 min)**:
   - *"This is the same math test you took on the very first day of the program. You're going to be amazed at how much more you can do now."*
   - *"Parts 1 and 3 are timed (2 minutes each). The rest is untimed — take your time and show your work."*
   - Distribute assessments. Administer:
     - Part 1: Addition/subtraction fact fluency — 2 minutes, as many as possible.
     - Part 2: Multi-digit addition and subtraction — untimed, 8 problems.
     - Part 3: Multiplication/division fact fluency — 2 minutes, as many as possible.
     - Part 4: Place value — 6 problems (value of digit, expanded form, comparison, rounding).
     - Part 5: Fractions — 6 problems (naming, comparing, number line, addition with like denominators for 4th).
   - No manipulatives during the assessment (this mirrors the pre-assessment conditions).

2. **Debrief (5 min)**:
   - *"How did that feel compared to Week 1?"*
   - *"What problems felt EASY that were hard before?"*
   - Collect assessments. Do not share scores yet — teacher will compare pre/post and present data in the final report.

#### Closure (5 min)
- *"Tomorrow we'll have more time for problem solving and review games. Your assessment today helps me measure your growth — and the numbers are going to be impressive."*
- **Math Growth Story Preview**: *"This week you'll create a 'My Math Growth Story' poster. It will show your journey from Week 1 to Week 6 — the strategies you learned, the problems you can solve now, and the data that proves your growth."*

#### Differentiation

For the post-assessment, provide the same test as Week 1 — no modifications (to ensure valid comparison). Allow extra time if needed for untimed sections. Note accommodations used.
- **On level (Group B)**: Full lesson and assessment as described.
- **Above level (Group C)**: During strategy review, add 2 challenge problems: a 3-step problem and one requiring fraction operations. Assessment is the same for all (to ensure valid data).

#### Data Collection

- Calculate growth: pre-score → post-score for each section.
- Flag any areas where a student scored LOWER than pre-assessment (investigate: fatigue? misunderstanding? re-assess later in the week?).

#### Part 1: OG Lesson — Soft c and Soft g (10:30–10:50)

**Objective**: Students will learn that c says /s/ before e, i, y and g says /j/ before e, i, y. They will read and sort words by hard/soft c and hard/soft g. (IEP Goal: Decoding — apply advanced phonics patterns to read unfamiliar words with >=80% accuracy.)

**Materials**:
- Soft c word cards: city, cent, circle, cycle, ice, face, place, race, peace, dance, prince, since, once, rice, nice, pencil, ceiling, certain
- Soft g word cards: gem, giant, gym, giraffe, page, cage, stage, age, large, change, orange, danger, magic, gentle, ginger
- Hard c word cards (for contrast): cat, cup, come, can, car, coat, corn, class
- Hard g word cards (for contrast): go, got, game, gas, girl, give, grow, green
- Sorting mat: Hard c | Soft c | Hard g | Soft g
- OG phonogram cards — cumulative deck
- Dry-erase boards + markers
- Sand/salt trays
- Red word note: "once" — this follows the soft c rule (c before e) but the vowel sound is irregular

**OG Lesson Structure**:

1. **Cumulative Review (3 min)**:
   - Quick phonogram drill: all vowel teams, r-controlled, digraphs.
   - Quick prefix/suffix flash: un-, re-, pre-, dis-, -ful, -less, -ness, -ly.
   - 3 red words from Week 5: beautiful, important, suddenly.

2. **New Concept — Soft c (7 min)**:
   - Write on the board: **cat, city, cup, cent, cut, cycle**
   - *"Read these words with me. Listen to the sound the letter C makes in each word."*
   - *"In 'cat,' 'cup,' and 'cut' — C says /k/. That's called HARD C."*
   - *"In 'city,' 'cent,' and 'cycle' — C says /s/. That's called SOFT C."*
   - *"Look at the letter that comes AFTER the c. In the hard c words, what vowels come after c?"* (a, u, u) *"In the soft c words?"* (i, e, y)
   - **Rule**: C says /s/ before e, i, or y. Otherwise, c says /k/.
   - Write on anchor chart: **C before e, i, y = /s/ (soft c)**
   - Practice: Teacher says a word, students give thumbs up for soft c, down for hard c:
     - circle (soft), class (hard), ceiling (soft), corn (hard), ice (soft), came (hard), pencil (soft).

3. **New Concept — Soft g (5 min)**:
   - Write: **go, gem, game, giant, get, gym**
   - *"Same idea! G before e, i, y usually says /j/ — that's soft g. Otherwise, g says /g/ — hard g."*
   - **Rule**: G before e, i, y = /j/ (soft g)
   - *"But be careful — soft g has more exceptions than soft c. 'Get,' 'give,' and 'girl' all have g before e or i but say /g/. We call those exceptions."*
   - Write on anchor chart: **G before e, i, y = /j/ (soft g) — but watch for exceptions!**
   - Practice thumbs up/down: giant (soft), game (hard), giraffe (soft), give (hard/exception), magic (soft), green (hard), orange (soft).

4. **Sorting Activity (3 min)**:
   - Pairs receive word cards. Sort into 4 piles: Hard c, Soft c, Hard g, Soft g.
   - 12 words to sort: city, car, gem, go, face, cup, stage, gas, nice, coat, ginger, girl.
   - Quick check as a class.

5. **Sand Tray + Dictation (2 min)**:
   - Dictate 3 soft c/g words: cent, page, circle.
   - Students write in sand tray, then transfer to dry-erase board.

#### Differentiation
- **Below level (Group A)**: Focus on soft c only today (soft g Tuesday). Use only high-frequency soft c words: city, ice, nice, face, place. Sort only 6 words (3 hard c, 3 soft c). Provide the rule on a reference card.
- **On level (Group B)**: Full lesson — both soft c and soft g. Sort 12 words. Dictation as described.
- **Above level (Group C)**: Full lesson plus: identify soft c and soft g words in a paragraph. Discuss exceptions for g (get, give, girl, begin). Challenge: *"Why do you think English has these rules? What pattern do the exceptions follow?"*

### Exploration & Enrichment (12:00–1:00)

**Activity**: Nature Reflection Walk — Comparing Week 1 to Week 6

**Overview**: Students revisit the nature walk from Week 1, Day 1. They compare their observations, vocabulary, and writing from then to now. This is a powerful visual demonstration of growth.

**Materials**:
- Nature journals from Week 1 (original entries)
- Fresh nature journal pages (or new section in the same journal)
- Pencils + colored pencils
- Clipboards
- Magnifying glasses
- Chart paper for "Then and Now" class comparison

**Schedule**:

1. **Introduction (8 min, inside)**:
   - *"On our very first day — July 7 — we went on a nature walk. You sketched what you saw, wrote describing words, and collected objects. Today, six weeks later, we're going to do the SAME walk."*
   - Pass out Week 1 nature journals. Students read their original entries silently.
   - *"In a moment, we'll go outside and observe the same spaces. But this time, I want you to notice: How has your writing changed? How has your vocabulary grown? What do you notice now that you didn't notice before?"*
   - Today's journal page heading: **"Week 6 — Nature Walk Reflection"**

2. **Nature Walk (20 min, outside)**:
   - Walk the same route as Week 1.
   - At the same 3–4 stopping points, pause with the SAME prompts:
     - Stop 1: *"Look down at the ground. Find 3 different things and sketch one."*
     - Stop 2: *"Look up. Write 2 describing words."*
     - Stop 3: *"Use your magnifying glass. Find something tiny and draw it BIG."*
     - Stop 4: *"Collect 3–5 natural objects."*
   - **NEW addition**: At each stop, also write: *"What I notice now that I didn't notice in Week 1."*
   - Teacher observes and notes: How much more detail do students include? How much more fluent is their writing? How much richer is their vocabulary?

3. **"Then and Now" Comparison (15 min, inside)**:
   - Students open to their Week 1 page and their Week 6 page side by side.
   - On a "Then and Now" page, they record:
     - **Week 1, I wrote**: (copy a sentence from their original entry)
     - **Week 6, I wrote**: (copy a sentence from today's entry)
     - **What changed**: (describe differences — more detail? bigger words? more sentences? neater handwriting?)
   - Class chart paper: *"What do we notice about our growth?"* Teacher records observations:
     - "I used more describing words."
     - "My sentences are longer."
     - "I noticed things I missed the first time."
     - "I could spell harder words."
     - "My handwriting is better."
   - *"THIS is evidence of your growth. This is what your families will see at the showcase."*

4. **Portfolio Connection (7 min)**:
   - The Week 1 and Week 6 nature journal pages become a portfolio pair — taped or stapled facing each other as a "before and after."
   - Students add this to their portfolio folders.
   - *"This is one of the most powerful things in your portfolio — it shows your growth in your OWN words."*

5. **Closing Circle (10 min)**:
   - *"How did it feel to see your Week 1 work next to today's?"*
   - Go-around: each student says one word about how they've grown.
   - Self-monitoring checklist — *"Rate yourself on today's checklist. Then look at your Week 1 checklist. Has your independence grown?"*
   - Preview tomorrow: *"Tomorrow we learn silent letters and homophones, continue assessments, and create a mini-museum of your summer learning!"*
   - **Remind**: *"Family Showcase is FRIDAY at 11:30 AM. Make sure your families know!"*
   - Dismissal at 1:00.

**Academic Connections**:
- **Writing**: Comparing descriptive writing across time — concrete evidence of writing growth.
- **Reading**: Vocabulary development — students recognize and use more complex words.
- **Math**: Potential measurement comparison (if students measured objects in Week 1 and again today).
- **SEL**: Self-awareness, growth mindset, pride in accomplishment.

**Differentiation**:
- **Below level**: Teacher reads their Week 1 entry aloud to them. "Then and Now" page has sentence frames: "In Week 1, I wrote ___. Now I can write ___. This shows I grew in ___." Pair with a peer for the comparison discussion.
- **On level**: Full activity as described. Write 2–3 comparison observations independently.
- **Above level**: Write a full paragraph comparing Week 1 and Week 6 observations. Identify specific skills that improved and connect them to lessons learned. This paragraph becomes an additional portfolio piece.
