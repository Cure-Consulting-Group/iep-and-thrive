# Week 6 — "Showcase & Celebrate"
## Theme: Showing What We've Learned

**Focus**: Advanced phonics patterns, fluency building, cumulative review, portfolio assembly, post-assessment, family showcase

---

## Weekly Learning Objectives

### Reading & Phonics (OG)
- Teach soft c and soft g patterns: c says /s/ before e, i, y (city, cent, cycle); g says /j/ before e, i, y (gem, giant, gym)
- Introduce silent letter patterns: kn (know, knee, knit), wr (write, wrap, wrist), gn (gnat, gnaw, sign), mb (climb, lamb, thumb)
- Explore homophones (there/their/they're, to/too/two, write/right, no/know) and homographs (lead, wind, tear, close)
- Fluency building: repeated reading of familiar passages, phrase reading (scooping phrases with eyes), prosody practice (reading with expression — emphasize punctuation cues)
- Cumulative OG review: spiral through ALL phonics patterns taught Weeks 1–6 (closed, open, VCe, vowel teams, r-controlled, C+le, syllable division, morphology, soft c/g, silent letters)
- Red words: cumulative review of all red words taught + student-specific words from their own writing (individualized)
- Final OG dictation: mixed patterns from all six weeks

### Comprehension
- Synthesis: connecting ideas across multiple texts read during the summer program
- Metacognitive reflection: "What reading strategies help me most?" — students identify their go-to strategies
- "How has my thinking changed?" — compare initial reading response journal entries (Week 1) to current responses
- Reading response: write about a favorite book or text from the summer and explain why it was meaningful
- Review all comprehension strategies taught: predict, monitor, retell, main idea, story elements, sequence, inference, author's purpose, summarize

### Writing
- Portfolio assembly: students select their best pieces from the summer (1 narrative, 1 opinion, 1 informational, plus choice)
- Revision and editing of selected portfolio pieces using editing checklists:
  - Capitalization (beginning of sentence, proper nouns)
  - End punctuation (period, question mark, exclamation point)
  - Spelling (check red words and pattern words)
  - Does it make sense? (read aloud to self or partner)
- Write a "Letter to My Future Self" or "Letter to My September Teacher" — reflecting on growth, strengths, and goals for the school year
- Showcase preparation: write a brief presentation script or talking points for Friday showcase ("This summer I learned... My favorite piece is... I am proud of...")
- Reflective writing: "What I Learned This Summer" — final portfolio piece

### Math
- Multi-step word problems using cumulative skills (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, fractions, measurement)
- Strategy toolkit review: draw a picture, make a table, work backward, use a number line, write an equation, look for a pattern
- Students solve problems and explain their reasoning (focus on "How did you solve it?" and "Is there another way?")
- Math portfolio assembly: select best work samples, graph of fact fluency growth, completed problem sets
- Showcase preparation: "My Math Growth Story" poster — students display their growth using data from weekly probes
- Math games and fluency celebration: fact fluency games, math bingo, measurement challenges

### Executive Function & SEL
- Reflection and self-assessment: "What strategies do I use now that I didn't use in Week 1?"
- Self-monitoring review: complete a final self-assessment using the daily checklist — compare independence now vs. Week 1
- Goal review: revisit the goals set in Week 1 — did we meet them? What do we still want to work on?
- Practice presentation skills: eye contact, clear voice, standing tall, answering audience questions
- SEL focus: celebrating growth (not just achievement), gratitude, and looking forward
- Goodbye rituals: signing each other's journals, writing compliments, creating a class memory book page

## Differentiation Notes

### Flexible Grouping — Week 6

This week, flexible groups shift from instructional level groups to showcase preparation groups. All students participate in cumulative review, but focus areas are personalized.

**Phonics Review — Individualized**:
- Each student receives a personalized "patterns to review" card based on their cumulative data — focusing on patterns where accuracy is below 80%
- Students who have mastered all patterns: serve as "phonics coaches" during partner review; focus on fluency and prosody rather than decoding accuracy
- Students with persistent gaps: intensive review of 2–3 target patterns daily; additional decodable text practice at their level; these patterns flagged in the final report for fall teacher

**Math Review — Individualized**:
- Each student's "Math Growth Story" poster reflects their personal journey — entry point, growth, and current level
- Multi-step word problems offered at 3 levels:
  - Level 1: 1-step problems using addition/subtraction/multiplication, with manipulatives available
  - Level 2: 2-step problems using mixed operations including division and simple fractions
  - Level 3: 2–3 step problems with fractions, measurement, and multiple operations; explain reasoning in writing
- All students participate in math games — games are self-differentiating (e.g., fact fluency bingo can use different fact ranges)

**Writing & Portfolio — Individualized**:
- All students assemble portfolios from their collected work
- Revision depth varies: some students focus on one piece for deep revision (conventions and clarity); others revise 2–3 pieces
- Showcase presentation: all students present, but format is flexible
  - Students who are comfortable: present to full audience with talking points
  - Students who prefer smaller settings: present to a small group or 1:1 with their family
  - Visual supports available for all: poster, portfolio pieces on display, cue cards

**Comprehension — Whole Group + Individual Reflection**:
- Cumulative strategy review is whole group (interactive anchor chart review)
- Individual reflection ("What strategies help me most?") is personalized
- Reading response about favorite text: students choose their own text and write at their level
