#### Part 1: Fluency Celebration — Repeated Reading + Prosody (10:30–10:45)

**Objective**: Students will demonstrate fluency growth through repeated reading and prosody practice. This is a celebration, not an assessment. (IEP Goal: Fluency — read with appropriate rate, accuracy, and prosody.)

**Materials**:
- Fluency passages (at each student's instructional level — pre-marked with phrase boundaries using scooping marks)
- Prosody rubric (NAEP scale — posted, simplified for students):
  - Level 1: Word-by-word reading
  - Level 2: Some 2-word phrases, mostly choppy
  - Level 3: Mostly smooth phrasing, some expression
  - Level 4: Smooth, expressive reading with natural phrasing
- Timer
- Audience (classmates)

**Activity — "Fluency Showcase Preview"**:

1. **Repeated Reading Practice (5 min)**:
   - Each student has a passage at their instructional level with phrase boundaries marked.
   - Practice reading the passage 3 times:
     - Read 1: Whisper read to yourself. Focus on accuracy.
     - Read 2: Read to your partner. Focus on phrasing (use the scoop marks).
     - Read 3: Read to your partner with EXPRESSION. Pretend you're reading to a younger child — make it interesting!

2. **Fluency Performances (10 min)**:
   - Volunteers read a portion of their passage aloud to the class.
   - After each performance, class gives specific feedback:
     - *"I noticed you paused at the comma — that's great phrasing!"*
     - *"You made the character's voice sound excited — that's prosody!"*
   - *"At the showcase tomorrow, your family might ask you to read something from your portfolio. Now you know how to read with expression!"*
   - Every student who reads aloud gets a round of applause.

#### Differentiation
- **Below level**: Passage is at their instructional level (may be below grade level). Phrase boundaries are clearly marked. Practice 4+ times before performing. Teacher reads with them (echo reading) during practice. No pressure to perform aloud — offer the option of reading to just the teacher.
- **On level**: Grade-level passage. 3 practice reads. Perform willingly.
- **Above level**: Grade-level or above passage. Focus on prosody — vary pace for suspense, emphasize key words, use character voices. After performing, self-assess on the NAEP rubric.

#### Part 2: Letter to My Future Self/Teacher (10:45–11:05)

**Objective**: Students will write a reflective letter demonstrating metacognitive awareness of their growth, strengths, and goals. This becomes a portfolio piece and a keepsake. (IEP Goal: Written Expression — write a reflective piece with clear ideas and personal voice.)

**Materials**:
- "Letter to My Future Self/Teacher" template (1 per student):
  - Heading: "Dear Future Me" OR "Dear [September Teacher's Name]"
  - Section 1: "This summer, I learned..."
  - Section 2: "I am proud of..."
  - Section 3: "A challenge I overcame was..."
  - Section 4: "The strategies that help me most are..."
  - Section 5: "My goal for the school year is..."
  - Closing: "Sincerely, [Name]"
- Lined paper (if students want to write without the template)
- Pencils, colored pencils for decoration

**Lesson**:

1. **Introduction (3 min)**:
   - *"Today you're writing a letter to either your future self — the 'you' who will read this in September — or to your new teacher. This letter tells them who you are as a learner and what you've accomplished this summer."*
   - *"This is one of the most important pieces in your portfolio. If you write to your teacher, this letter helps them understand what you need and what you can do."*
   - Read a teacher-written model letter (brief, on chart paper).

2. **Writing Time (15 min)**:
   - Students write using the template or freely.
   - Teacher circulates quietly:
     - *"What's the most important thing your teacher should know about you?"*
     - *"What strategy do you want to make sure you keep using?"*
     - *"What are you most proud of?"*
   - Encourage honesty and specificity: not "I learned math" but "I learned how to compare fractions using benchmarks and how to solve 2-step word problems."
   - Students who finish early: decorate the border of their letter, or write a second letter (if they wrote to themselves, also write to their teacher, or vice versa).

3. **Optional Sharing (2 min)**:
   - 1–2 volunteers read a portion of their letter. Applause.
   - *"These letters go in your portfolio. Tomorrow your family can read them."*

#### Differentiation
- **Below level**: Use the template as a fill-in. Teacher conferences for 3 minutes to help brainstorm ideas. Accept shorter responses (1–2 sentences per section). May dictate portions. Focus on Sections 1 and 2 (learned and proud) if time is limited.
- **On level**: Use the template as a guide, but write in full letter format. Complete all 5 sections with 2+ sentences each.
- **Above level**: Write freely without the template. Include specific data from their growth card. Address the letter to their September teacher with specific recommendations: *"I work best when ___, and I sometimes struggle with ___, but the strategy that helps is ___."* This becomes a powerful self-advocacy document.

#### Part 3: Portfolio Finalization + Self-Assessment (11:05–11:30)

**Objective**: Students will complete their portfolio assembly, including a self-assessment reflection, and ensure all pieces are organized for the showcase. (IEP Goal: Executive Function — organize and complete a multi-step project independently.)

**Materials**:
- Portfolio folders with all collected work
- Portfolio assembly checklist (from Monday — updated)
- Portfolio self-assessment form (1 per student):
  - Rate yourself (1–4) on each area:
    - Reading/Decoding: "I can break apart big words and read them."
    - Reading Fluency: "I can read smoothly with expression."
    - Reading Comprehension: "I can understand what I read and answer questions about it."
    - Writing: "I can write paragraphs with details and correct spelling."
    - Math Computation: "I can solve multiplication, division, and fraction problems."
    - Math Problem Solving: "I can read word problems and figure out what to do."
    - Executive Function: "I can stay organized, follow directions, and ask for help."
  - For each rating, write: "Evidence: ___" (What proves this? Name a specific assignment or skill.)
  - Final reflection: "My biggest growth area this summer was ___."
  - "One thing I still want to work on is ___."
- Sticky notes, tabs, tape
- Clear sheet protectors (if available — for the cover page)

**Activity**:

1. **Portfolio Self-Assessment (10 min)**:
   - Students complete the self-assessment form thoughtfully.
   - *"Be honest with yourself. A '2' with a plan to grow is better than a '4' that isn't true."*
   - Teacher circulates: *"What evidence do you have for that rating? Can you point to a specific piece in your portfolio?"*
   - This form goes at the FRONT of the portfolio — families see it first.

2. **Final Organization (15 min)**:
   - Students use the checklist to verify all pieces are present:
     - [ ] Portfolio self-assessment (front)
     - [ ] Growth data card (teacher-provided — attached inside front cover)
     - [ ] Week 1 writing sample (diagnostic — marked "Week 1")
     - [ ] Week 6 writing sample (post-assessment — marked "Week 6")
     - [ ] Narrative piece (revised)
     - [ ] Opinion essay "Why ___ Matters" (final)
     - [ ] Informational piece (if completed)
     - [ ] Reading response journal entries (3 best, marked with sticky tabs)
     - [ ] Reading strategy toolkit reflection
     - [ ] Favorite text response
     - [ ] Letter to My Future Self/Teacher
     - [ ] Math work samples (2–3, marked with growth annotations)
     - [ ] Nature walk comparison (Week 1 vs. Week 6)
     - [ ] Any enrichment project photos or work
   - Organize in chronological order or by subject — student's choice.
   - Add a cover page: Name, "My Summer Learning Portfolio," "IEP & Thrive, Summer 2026"
   - Decorate the cover page (if time allows).

3. **Portfolio Check (5 min)**:
   - Teacher does a quick walk-through: checks each portfolio for completeness.
   - Any missing pieces: teacher locates them or notes for the student to add tomorrow morning.
   - *"Your portfolio is DONE. Tomorrow your family will hold this in their hands and see everything you've accomplished."*

#### Differentiation
- **Below level**: Self-assessment done verbally with teacher (teacher records responses). Portfolio organization assisted by teacher/aide — pieces may be pre-sorted; student arranges and verifies. Cover page: write name and decorate (title may be pre-printed).
- **On level**: Full self-assessment and portfolio assembly independently. Cover page designed independently.
- **Above level**: Self-assessment includes detailed written evidence for each rating. Portfolio includes a one-page "Portfolio Introduction" — a written guide for the reader explaining what's inside and what the student is most proud of.

### Exploration & Enrichment (12:00–1:00)

**Activity**: Plan & "Host" a Celebration + Showcase Rehearsal

**Overview**: Students plan the logistics of Friday's showcase (life skills: event planning) and then rehearse their presentations.

**Materials**:
- Showcase schedule (printed — for review with students)
- Showcase presentation script template:
  - "Welcome to my exhibit! My name is ___ and I'm in ___ grade."
  - "This summer at IEP & Thrive, I learned ___."
  - "My favorite project was ___."
  - "I am most proud of ___."
  - "Let me show you my portfolio. This piece is ___ and it shows ___."
  - "Thank you for coming to our showcase!"
- Cue cards (index cards — for presentation notes)
- Timer (for timed practice)
- Celebration planning worksheet: task list for Friday setup
- Decorations: streamers, construction paper, markers, tape
- Certificates of completion (sealed — teacher prepared)
- Compliment cards/sticky notes
- Class memory book page template

**Schedule**:

1. **Showcase Logistics Review (5 min)**:
   - Review the Friday schedule (from the overview):

   | Time | Activity |
   |------|---------|
   | 9:00–9:30 | Final morning meeting + group photo |
   | 9:30–10:30 | Portfolio and exhibit setup |
   | 10:30–11:00 | Presentation rehearsal |
   | 11:00–11:30 | Student-only celebration (awards, compliments, memory book) |
   | 11:30–11:45 | Welcome families + program overview |
   | 11:45–12:30 | Student presentations + gallery walk |
   | 12:30–12:45 | Certificate ceremony |
   | 12:45–1:00 | Closing + refreshments |

   - Assign setup tasks for tomorrow morning: Who arranges tables? Who puts up decorations? Who sets out portfolios?

2. **Presentation Script Writing (10 min)**:
   - Students write their talking points on cue cards using the script template.
   - *"You don't have to memorize a speech. Just have 5–6 talking points on your cards that you can glance at."*
   - Teacher circulates helping students identify what to highlight.

3. **Rehearsal — Partner Practice (10 min)**:
   - Students pair up. Each student presents to their partner (2 minutes each).
   - Partner gives feedback: *"I could hear you clearly. I think you should also mention ___."*
   - Switch partners. Practice again with a new audience.
   - Teacher coaches presentation skills: *"Stand up straight. Speak loudly enough for the back of the room. Look at your audience, not just your cards. It's okay to be nervous — everyone is."*

4. **Rehearsal — Full Group (10 min)**:
   - 3–4 volunteers do a full rehearsal in front of the class.
   - Class practices being a supportive audience: sit quietly, listen, applaud at the end.
   - Quick feedback after each: one positive comment per performance.
   - *"Tomorrow, there will be more people in the room. But the most important person you're presenting to is YOURSELF — showing yourself what you've accomplished."*

5. **Celebration Planning (10 min)**:
   - **Decoration creation**: Students make "Welcome to Our Showcase" banner and table signs.
   - **Compliment circle**: Each student writes one compliment on a sticky note for every classmate. Fold and place in a cup/bag with that student's name. (These will be given out tomorrow during the student-only celebration.)
   - **Class memory book page**: Each student fills in a template:
     - My name: ___
     - My favorite memory from this summer: ___
     - A friend I made: ___
     - Something funny that happened: ___
     - A drawing of me at IEP & Thrive:
   - Collect all pages — teacher assembles into a class memory book tonight (photocopy for each student to take home Friday).

6. **Closing Circle — Penultimate Day (15 min)**:
   - *"Tomorrow is our last day. Before we go, I want to tell you something."*
   - Teacher shares a personal reflection: *"When you walked in 6 weeks ago, you were brave. You came to a new place, with new people, and you trusted us with your learning. Now look at what you've built — portfolios full of writing, math posters with real data, museum exhibits, friendships. I am so proud of each of you."*
   - **Journal signing**: Students pass journals around the circle. Each person writes their name and a short message in every classmate's journal. (10 min — play background music.)
   - *"These journals are yours to keep forever."*
   - Self-monitoring checklist — FINAL regular check.
   - *"Tomorrow: arrive at 9:00 as usual. Families come at 11:30. Wear something you feel confident in. Bring your smile and your courage. You've got this."*
   - **Send home**: Printed showcase schedule for families, class memory book page (original — copy will come Friday), reminder note about 11:30 family arrival time.
   - Dismissal at 1:00.

**Differentiation**:
- **Below level**: Presentation script is shorter (3–4 talking points). Practice with teacher 1:1 before group rehearsal. Cue cards may have pictures alongside words. Option to present to small group or just family (not full audience). Decoration tasks: concrete, hands-on (cutting letters, coloring).
- **On level**: Full script with 5–6 talking points. Practice with partner and group. Create decorations.
- **Above level**: Script includes specific data references and connects learning to future goals. Practice without cue cards (use them as backup only). Help peers rehearse. Lead decoration creation for the group.
