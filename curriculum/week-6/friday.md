### Final Morning Meeting (9:00–9:30)

**This is the last morning circle. Make it meaningful.**

1. **Feelings Check-In (3 min)**:
   - *"This is our very last morning circle. Take a deep breath. How are you feeling?"*
   - Go around the circle. Every student shares a word or sentence. Teacher validates each one.
   - Common feelings: excited, nervous, sad, proud, happy, grateful, scared, ready.
   - *"Every single one of those feelings is exactly right."*

2. **Week 1 Goals Revisit (5 min)**:
   - Open journals to Week 1, Day 1: "One thing I want to get better at this summer is ___."
   - Teacher reads each student's Week 1 goal aloud (with permission). After each, the class responds with evidence:
     - Student's Week 1 goal: *"I want to get better at reading big words."*
     - Teacher: *"And now you can break apart words like 'unhappiness' and 'disagreement' using morphemes. You did it!"*
     - Classmates: spontaneous applause or snaps.
   - Continue for each student (keep it moving — 15–20 seconds per student).

3. **Teacher's Words (5 min)**:
   - *"Six weeks ago, you sat in these same chairs. Some of you were nervous. Some of you weren't sure this program was going to work. And every single one of you showed up, worked hard, tried new things, and grew."*
   - *"In this room, you learned [number] phonics patterns, [number] red words, how to write narratives, opinions, and informational pieces, how to multiply, divide, and compare fractions, and how to believe in yourselves as learners."*
   - *"But the thing I'm most proud of isn't on any test. It's that you learned to try when things are hard, to ask for help, to help each other, and to keep going. That's what you're taking into September — not just skills, but confidence."*
   - *"Today we celebrate YOU."*

4. **Group Photo (5 min)**:
   - Whole class photo: students + staff.
   - Silly photo.
   - Serious photo.
   - Individual photos at their stations (take during setup time too).

5. **Acknowledgments (5 min)**:
   - *"Before we set up, I want to acknowledge some things."*
   - Acknowledge each student individually with one specific, personal observation:
     - *"[Name], you came in reading at ___ and today you read at ___. But what I'll remember most is the day you helped [other student] figure out that fraction problem instead of just giving the answer."*
     - *"[Name], your opinion essay about ___ was one of the strongest pieces of writing in this room. You found your voice this summer."*
     - Continue for each student (15–20 seconds each).
   - *"You are ALL ready for September."*

### Presentation Rehearsal — Final (10:30–11:00)

**This is the last chance to practice before families arrive.**

1. **Quick Warm-Up (3 min)**:
   - *"Let's warm up our voices and our confidence."*
   - Deep breathing: breathe in for 4, hold for 4, out for 4. Repeat 3 times.
   - Power pose: Stand tall, hands on hips, chin up. Hold for 30 seconds. *"This is how a confident presenter stands."*
   - Vocal warm-up: Say "I am proud of my learning" loudly, then at a normal volume, then in a whisper. *"At the showcase, use your 'normal to loud' voice."*

2. **Full Rehearsal (20 min)**:
   - Each student stands at their station and delivers their presentation (1–2 minutes) as if families were there.
   - The rest of the class walks around as "pretend families," stopping at each station, listening, and asking one question.
   - Teacher provides final coaching:
     - *"Speak a little louder — I couldn't hear your second point."*
     - *"Great job making eye contact!"*
     - *"Remember to point to your portfolio when you mention your writing."*
     - *"You don't have to be perfect. Just be yourself and be proud."*
   - Students who are very nervous: practice presenting just to the teacher or to one friend. Reassure them that presenting to their own family is different — *"Your family already loves you. They're coming to celebrate, not to judge."*

3. **Final Reminders (7 min)**:
   - *"When families arrive at 11:30, stay at your station. I'll welcome everyone and give a brief overview. Then families will come to YOUR station and you'll present."*
   - *"If you forget what to say, look at your cue cards. If you get nervous, take a breath. If you feel proud, smile — because you SHOULD."*
   - *"After all presentations, we'll have the certificate ceremony. Then refreshments and goodbyes."*
   - *"Any last questions?"*

**Differentiation**:
- **Students who are anxious about presenting to a large group**: Arrange for them to present only to their own family members at their station (not to the full audience). Teacher checks in 1:1 before families arrive.
- **Students who are very confident**: May volunteer to be "ambassadors" — greeting families at the door and guiding them to the first station.
- **Students with speech/language needs**: Cue cards with full sentences (not just bullet points). Option to read directly from cards. No penalty for reading vs. speaking from memory.
- **All students**: Visual timer visible so they know how much time they have. Teacher circulates during gallery walk to prompt or support as needed.

#### Student Presentations + Gallery Walk (11:45–12:30)

**Structure**: Families go directly to their child's station first. The student presents their learning (1–3 minutes). Then families browse other stations during the gallery walk.

**At Each Station, Students Present**:

Using their cue cards, students walk their family through:

1. **Welcome**: *"Hi! Welcome to my exhibit. I'm [name] and I just finished 6 weeks at IEP & Thrive."*

2. **My Mini-Museum Exhibit**: Walk family through the display board — explain each item and what it represents.

3. **My Portfolio**: Open and walk through key pieces:
   - *"This is my writing from Week 1 [show diagnostic]. And THIS is my writing from Week 6 [show post-assessment]. Do you see the difference?"*
   - *"This is my opinion essay about [topic]. I wrote it all by myself."*
   - *"This is my letter to my future self [or September teacher]. It talks about what I learned and what strategies help me."*

4. **My Math Growth Story**: Point to the poster:
   - *"In Week 1, I scored ___ on my math test. Now I scored ___. That's ___ points of growth."*
   - *"The strategies I learned are ___."*

5. **What I'm Most Proud Of**: Share one personal highlight.

6. **Thank You**: *"Thank you for coming to our showcase!"*

**Teacher's Role During Gallery Walk**:
- Circulate among stations. Support students who are struggling or frozen (stand nearby, prompt with a question, or gently start: *"Tell your mom about your opinion essay."*)
- Take photos of students presenting to families.
- Engage with families: answer questions, share observations, highlight specific student achievements.
- Ensure every student gets at least one interaction (if a student's family couldn't attend, teacher or staff member serves as their audience — this must be planned in advance).

**For Students Whose Families Could Not Attend**:
- This MUST be anticipated and planned. The teacher or a staff member, volunteer, or another student's family member serves as the student's audience.
- The student still presents. Their work is still displayed. They still receive their certificate.
- *"Your family couldn't be here today, but your work is still incredible. Let me be your audience."*
- Teacher may also take a video of the student's presentation to share with the family.

**Gallery Walk** (12:00–12:30):
- After families visit their own child's station, they're encouraged to walk around and see other students' work.
- Students may stay at their station to answer questions from visiting families, or they may walk with their families.
- Quiet background music playing.

### Differentiation Notes for Showcase Day

**For students with anxiety about presenting**:
- Pre-arrange with the family: parent comes early (11:15) for a private 1:1 presentation before the group event begins.
- Student presents to family only at their station — does not need to present to the full room.
- Cue cards have full sentences, not just bullet points.
- Teacher stands nearby as a supportive presence during the presentation.
- Backup plan: If the student freezes, teacher can gently co-present: *"[Name], tell your mom about your opinion essay. You wrote about ___, right?"*

**For students with behavioral needs**:
- Clear expectations reviewed in morning meeting: *"Today is special. We're showing our best selves."*
- Give a specific job (greeter, tech helper, refreshment setup) to channel energy.
- Provide a quiet space/break area if overstimulated.
- Check in privately before families arrive: *"How are you feeling? What do you need?"*

**For students whose families cannot attend**:
- MUST be planned in advance — never let a child feel their work wasn't seen.
- Assign a staff member, volunteer, or another family as their audience.
- Video record their presentation to share with their family.
- Teacher presents their certificate with full fanfare: *"Your family asked me to tell you how proud they are."*
- Consider a phone/video call during the showcase so the family can watch live.

**For English Language Learners or students with communication differences**:
- Cue cards in both languages if applicable.
- Allow use of visual aids (pointing to items rather than describing verbally).
- Presentation may be shorter.
- Celebrate effort and participation, not fluency.
