### Morning Warm-Up (9:00–9:20)

**Community Check-In: "Assessment Day — You've Got This"**

1. **Feelings Check-In (3 min)**: *"Today we have our last assessments. How do you feel about that?"* Validate all feelings. *"Remember — these assessments measure your GROWTH. They're not about being perfect. They're about showing how far you've come."*

2. **Cumulative Phonogram Speed Drill (5 min)**:
   - Full deck — ALL phonogram cards from Weeks 1–6: consonants, short vowels, digraphs (sh, ch, th, wh, ck), blends, vowel teams (ai, ay, ee, ea, oa, ow, oi, oy, ou, ow, au, aw), r-controlled (ar, or, er, ir, ur), soft c, soft g.
   - Flash rapidly. Students respond chorally.
   - Then: 5 silent letter words (know, write, gnat, climb, wrong) — students read and identify the silent letter.
   - Then: 5 homophone sentences on the board — students write the correct word on boards.

3. **Prefix/Suffix Speed Round (5 min)**:
   - Teacher says a word. Students break it into morphemes on boards:
     - "unhappiness" → un + happy + ness
     - "disagreeably" → dis + agree + ably
     - "rebuilding" → re + build + ing
     - "preschooler" → pre + school + er
     - "carelessly" → care + less + ly
   - *"You can break apart BIG words now. That's a superpower you didn't have 6 weeks ago."*

4. **Assessment Pep Talk (7 min)**:
   - *"Today you'll take the writing assessment, comprehension assessment, and math concepts assessment. These are the SAME tests you took in Week 1."*
   - *"Here's what I want you to remember: In Week 1, many of these questions were brand new. Now you have 6 weeks of strategies and practice. Use everything you've learned."*
   - Quick strategy reminders:
     - **Writing**: Use the planning steps — topic sentence, details, closing. Check CUPS when done.
     - **Comprehension**: Use "I read... I know... So I think..." for inference questions. Look back at the text.
     - **Math**: Use your strategy toolkit. Show your work. Draw a picture if it helps.
   - *"I believe in every single one of you. Let's show what we've learned."*

#### Part 1: Cumulative OG Review (10:30–10:40)

**Objective**: Quick cumulative review of ALL phonics patterns before the writing assessment (priming spelling and decoding). (IEP Goal: Decoding — demonstrate mastery of phonics patterns taught across 6 weeks.)

**Materials**:
- OG phonogram cards — complete deck
- Syllable type reference chart
- Morpheme cards
- Dry-erase boards

**Quick Review Activities**:

1. **Syllable Type Sort (3 min)**:
   - Teacher reads 6 words. Students identify the syllable type on boards: closed, open, VCe, vowel team, r-controlled, consonant-le.
   - Words: "stamp" (closed), "go" (open), "shake" (VCe), "train" (vowel team), "barn" (r-controlled), "table" (C+le).

2. **Phonogram Flash — Lightning Round (3 min)**:
   - Flash 20 cards in 2 minutes. Students respond chorally.
   - Include all key patterns: sh, ch, th, ai, ea, oa, oi, ar, or, er, aw, ow (both sounds), soft c, soft g.

3. **Morpheme Breakdown (2 min)**:
   - 3 complex words on the board. Students break apart on boards:
   - "unbreakable" → un + break + able
   - "disagreement" → dis + agree + ment
   - "hopefully" → hope + ful + ly

4. **Red Word Quick Write (2 min)**:
   - Flash 5 commonly missed red words (based on teacher's data from the week). Students write from memory.

### Exploration & Enrichment (12:00–1:00)

**Activity**: Final STEM Challenge — Combine All Skills

**Overview**: The culminating STEM challenge requires students to combine literacy, math, and design thinking skills from the entire summer program. This is the "capstone" enrichment activity.

**Materials**:
- Per group (3 groups of 3–4 students):
  - Full range of building materials: cardboard, craft sticks, tape, string, straws, foil, rubber bands, recyclables, scissors, rulers
  - Measuring tools: rulers, measuring tapes, protractors
  - Writing materials: paper, markers, index cards
  - Calculators (for checking)
- Challenge description cards (1 per group)
- Presentation planning sheet
- Timer

**The Challenge: "Design a Community Space"**

*"Your team has been hired to design a small community space — a mini-park, reading nook, garden, or playground. You must build a model, create a budget, write a persuasive description of why your community needs this space, and present your design to the class."*

**Requirements**:
1. **Build**: A 3D model of your community space using the provided materials (Design Thinking + STEM skills)
2. **Measure**: Your model must include at least 3 measurements labeled in inches or centimeters (Math — measurement skills)
3. **Budget**: Create a pretend budget for building the real version — list 5 items and their costs, calculate the total, show that it's under $500 (Math — money and computation)
4. **Write**: A persuasive description (3–5 sentences) explaining why the community needs this space and who would benefit (Writing — opinion/persuasive skills)
5. **Present**: Each team member speaks for at least 30 seconds during the presentation (Communication + SEL)

**Schedule**:

1. **Challenge Introduction (5 min)**:
   - Present the challenge. Read requirements together.
   - *"This challenge uses EVERYTHING you've learned: measurement, budgeting, persuasive writing, design thinking, teamwork, and presentation skills."*
   - Assign groups (intentionally mixed by ability level).

2. **Planning Phase (10 min)**:
   - Groups discuss: What kind of community space? Who is it for?
   - Sketch the design. Assign roles: Builder(s), Measurer, Budget Writer, Persuasive Writer.
   - List materials needed.

3. **Build & Create Phase (20 min)**:
   - Groups build their models.
   - Simultaneously: one student writes the budget, another writes the persuasive description.
   - Measurer uses ruler to measure model dimensions and labels them on index cards.
   - Teacher circulates:
     - *"How did you decide on this design?"*
     - *"Is your budget under $500? Double-check your addition."*
     - *"Does your persuasive description have a claim and reasons?"*
   - 5-minute warning.

4. **Presentations (15 min — 5 min per group)**:
   - Each group presents:
     - Show the model and explain the design choices.
     - Share measurements.
     - Read the budget (total cost).
     - Read the persuasive description.
   - Audience asks 1–2 questions after each presentation.
   - Teacher highlights connections: *"Notice how [team] used measurement skills, budgeting, AND persuasive writing — all in one project. That's what real-world problem solvers do!"*

5. **Closing Circle (10 min)**:
   - *"This was our final STEM challenge. You just combined reading, writing, math, design, and teamwork into one project. THAT is what 6 weeks of learning looks like."*
   - Each student shares: *"The skill I used most today was ___."*
   - Self-monitoring checklist — final Wednesday check.
   - **Tomorrow preview**: *"Tomorrow is showcase rehearsal day. We'll finish our posters, practice our presentations, write our letters, and get everything ready for Friday. I'll also share your assessment growth data — get ready to be impressed!"*
   - Dismissal at 1:00.

**Differentiation**:
- **Below level**: Place in a group with supportive peers. Assign the "Measurer" role (concrete, hands-on). Budget: provide a template with items pre-listed — student fills in prices and adds. Persuasive writing: contribute 1–2 sentences verbally, peer writes them down. During presentation: read one sentence or describe one part of the model.
- **On level**: Full participation in all requirements. Write 3+ sentences for persuasive description. Present for 30+ seconds.
- **Above level**: Lead the design discussion. Budget includes unit pricing (e.g., "10 benches at $25 each = $250"). Persuasive description addresses a counterargument. Present for 60+ seconds and answer audience questions independently.
