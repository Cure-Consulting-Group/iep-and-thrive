**Brain Game: "Week in Review Trivia" (8 min)**:
- Quiz-style review of the week, connecting all subjects:
  - *"What are the two parts of a sentence called?"* (subject and predicate)
  - *"When two letters make ONE sound, like 'sh,' what do we call that?"* (a digraph)
  - *"What is the value of the 4 in the number 3,482?"* (400)
  - *"What does CUBES stand for in our word problem strategy?"* (Circle, Underline, Box, Evaluate, Solve)
  - *"Name one of our red words."* (the, was, is, are, have, come, some, one)
  - *"What is a closed syllable?"* (a syllable that ends in a consonant — the vowel says its short sound)

**Movement Activity: "Freeze Dance" (3 min)**:
- Play upbeat music. Students dance. When the music stops, freeze. Anyone still moving does 3 jumping jacks before the music restarts.
- Quick, fun energy boost before a review-heavy morning.

### Math Block (9:20–10:20)

**Objective**: Students will demonstrate understanding of place value, number comparison, rounding, and addition/subtraction through review activities, showing consolidation of Week 1 math concepts. (IEP Goal: Number Sense and Computation — review and apply.)

**Materials**:
- Station materials (see below for each station)
- Dry-erase boards + markers
- Base-ten blocks, number lines, place value charts (all available)
- "Math Week 1 Review" sheet (1 per student — 10 problems covering all Week 1 skills)
- Timer for station rotations
- Chart paper for "What We Learned in Math This Week" anchor chart

#### Warm-Up/Activation (5 min)
- **"Number Talk"**: Write on the board: **48 + 35**
- *"Solve this in your head. When you have an answer, put your thumb on your chest (not up in the air — this isn't a race)."*
- Wait for most thumbs. Then ask: *"Who wants to share how they solved it?"*
- Record different strategies on the board:
  - "I did 48 + 30 = 78, then 78 + 5 = 83."
  - "I did 50 + 35 = 85, then took away 2 = 83."
  - "I added 8 + 5 = 13, carried the 1, 4 + 3 + 1 = 8. Answer: 83."
- *"All of these strategies work. You can choose the one that makes the most sense to YOUR brain."*

#### Guided Practice — Math Stations (40 min total: 4 stations x 10 min each)

**Station Setup**: Divide students into 4 groups (these can be preliminary math groups based on your data so far, or mixed-ability groups for the review day). Each group rotates through all 4 stations.

**Station 1: Place Value Build It (Teacher-facilitated when possible)**
- Materials: Base-ten blocks, place value charts, task cards
- Task cards have a number in words (e.g., "four thousand, two hundred sixteen"). Students build it with blocks and write it in standard form and expanded form on dry-erase boards.
- 6 task cards, increasing difficulty:
  1. "three hundred forty-five" → 345
  2. "one thousand, eight hundred two" → 1,802 (note the zero)
  3. "five thousand, sixty" → 5,060 (two zeros)
  4. "nine thousand, nine hundred ninety-nine" → 9,999
  5. "two thousand, four hundred" → 2,400
  6. (4th grade) "twenty-three thousand, five hundred twelve" → 23,512

**Station 2: Comparison & Rounding Game**
- Materials: Deck of number cards (4-digit numbers), comparison symbols on cards (>, <, =), rounding chart
- Game: "War with a Twist" — two students each flip a card. Both students write the comparison (e.g., 3,482 > 2,917) on their dry-erase boards. If they agree, the student with the larger number keeps both cards. If they disagree, they discuss until they agree.
- After 5 rounds, each student rounds their 3 favorite numbers to the nearest 100 and writes them down.

**Station 3: Addition & Subtraction Practice**
- Materials: Word problem task cards (6 cards, from Thursday's set plus new ones), recording sheet, base-ten blocks available
- Students choose 3 word problems and solve using CUBES. Must show a model (drawing, bar model, or number line) for at least 2.
- Self-checking: answer key in an envelope. Students check their own work and correct errors in a different color.

**Station 4: Math Fact Fluency — Games**
- Materials: Addition/subtraction flashcards, dice (2 per pair), "Target Number" game board
- Game option 1: "Flashcard Challenge" — partner quiz. Keep a tally of facts known vs. facts to practice.
- Game option 2: "Target Number" — roll 2 dice, add or subtract to try to reach a target number (teacher sets the target: 50, then 100).
- Game option 3: "Salute" — three players. Two players each hold a card to their forehead (they can't see their own). The third player says the sum. Each player must figure out their own number.

**Rotation Schedule**:
- 9:25–9:35: Rotation 1
- 9:35–9:45: Rotation 2
- 9:45–9:55: Rotation 3
- 9:55–10:05: Rotation 4
- 10:05–10:15: Whole-group wrap-up

#### Closure (10 min)
- **"What We Learned in Math This Week" Anchor Chart**:
  - Teacher facilitates: *"What math ideas did we explore this week?"*
  - Students contribute; teacher writes on chart paper:
    - Place value — each digit has a value based on its position
    - Number lines help us compare and round
    - Addition and subtraction with regrouping
    - Word problems: use CUBES to attack them
    - Estimation helps us check our work
  - Post the anchor chart.

- **Math Week 1 Review Sheet** (take-home — NOT an assessment):
  - 10 problems covering all skills. Students start in class; finish at home if needed.
  - This goes in their Friday folder alongside the parent communication letter.

#### Data Collection
- Station observations: note student performance at each station on a clipboard roster
  - Place Value: Who built numbers correctly? Who struggled with zeros?
  - Comparison: Who used symbols correctly? Who could round?
  - Word Problems: Who used CUBES and models? Who jumped to computation?
  - Fact Fluency: Approximate speed and accuracy for basic facts
- These observations supplement (not replace) the formal assessment data from earlier this week
- Use this data to confirm or adjust preliminary group placements made last night

#### Differentiation
- Station groups can be leveled or mixed. For Friday review, mixed-ability groups work well — stronger students can support peers.
- Station 1 task cards are tiered (students choose their level or teacher assigns).
- Station 3 word problems are color-coded by difficulty (green = 2-digit, yellow = 3-digit, red = multi-step).
- Station 4 games are inherently differentiated (facts each student practices are at their own level).

#### Part 1: OG Cumulative Review — "Phonics Olympics" (10:30–11:00)

**Objective**: Students will demonstrate phonics knowledge from Week 1 (consonant sounds, short vowels, blends, digraphs, CVC/CCVC words, closed syllables, and 8 red words) through a multi-event review game. (IEP Goal: Decoding — cumulative review of Week 1 phonics patterns.)

**Materials**:
- OG phonogram cards (full Week 1 deck)
- Dry-erase boards + markers
- Sand/salt trays
- Word list: 20 review words (mix of CVC, blends, digraphs)
- Red word cards (all 8)
- "Phonics Olympics" score sheet (class-wide, posted on board)
- Stickers or stamps for participation

**Events**:

1. **Event 1: "Speed Sounds" (5 min)**:
   - Flash phonogram cards. Whole class responds chorally.
   - Then: individual challenge. Go around the room — each student gets one card. If they say the sound correctly within 3 seconds, they earn a point for the class. (This is collaborative, not competitive — the class works together to beat the target.)
   - Target: 80% accuracy as a class. If they hit it, everyone earns $2 in Thrive Bucks.

2. **Event 2: "Red Word Relay" (5 min)**:
   - Divide into 2 teams. Teams line up.
   - Teacher flashes a red word card. First person in each line writes it on the dry-erase board. If correct, earn a point. Rotate to the back.
   - Go through all 8 red words twice. Count team points.
   - Both teams earn $1 per correct word for their individual wallets.

3. **Event 3: "Word Building" (8 min)**:
   - Teacher says a word. Students build it on their dry-erase boards, then modify it.
   - Chain: **cat → chat → chap → chip → ship → shin → thin → than → that**
   - At each step, ask: *"What did I change? What letter or letters did you switch?"*
   - This is a classic OG word-chaining activity. It builds phonemic awareness and decoding simultaneously.
   - Do a second chain: **duck → stuck → stock → stop → shop → shot → shut → shrug**

4. **Event 4: "Dictation Sprint" (7 min)**:
   - Teacher dictates 8 words. Students write on dry-erase boards. After each word, hold up boards.
   - Words: **chop, blast, whip, trunk, shelf, stuck, shrimp, spend**
   - Score individually (teacher tallies on roster). This is informal progress data — not a formal assessment.
   - Students self-correct in a different color marker if they made an error.

5. **Celebration (5 min)**:
   - Count class points. Celebrate regardless of score: *"You just reviewed an entire week of phonics in 25 minutes. That's incredible."*
   - Award Thrive Bucks.
   - Quick reflection: *"Which event was easiest? Which was hardest? What do you still want to practice?"*

**Data Collection**:
- Individual dictation sprint scores (8 words — record per student)
- Red word relay: note which words are still challenging for specific students
- Word chaining: note who could keep up with the chain vs. who fell behind (indicates blending fluency)

#### Part 2: Journal Writing + Reading Response (11:00–11:30)

**Objective**: Students will write a journal entry of at least 5 sentences reflecting on their first week, using correct sentence structure (subject + predicate), capitalization, and end punctuation. (IEP Goal: Written Expression — sentence writing with conventions.)

**Materials**:
- Learning journals
- Pencils
- "Sentence Checklist" mini-poster (posted):
  - Does every sentence start with a capital letter?
  - Does every sentence end with . or ! or ?
  - Does every sentence have a subject and a predicate?
  - Did I spell my red words correctly?
- A short read-aloud book (optional — see below)

**Lesson**:

1. **Read-Aloud Connection (8 min)** — optional but recommended:
   - Read a short picture book about learning, growth, or perseverance. Suggestions:
     - *"The Most Magnificent Thing"* by Ashley Spires (about persistence in building — connects to STEM day)
     - *"Your Fantastic Elastic Brain"* by JoAnn Deak (about brain growth and learning)
     - *"Ish"* by Peter H. Reynolds (about doing things imperfectly and that being okay)
   - After reading, ask: *"What was the message of this book? How does it connect to our first week?"*
   - This sets the tone for reflective writing.

2. **Journal Writing (17 min)**:
   - Prompt (posted on board):

   > **"My First Week at IEP & Thrive"**
   > Write about your first week. You can answer any or all of these questions:
   > - What was your favorite activity?
   > - What did you learn that was new?
   > - What was hard? What was easy?
   > - What are you looking forward to next week?
   > - What do you want to get better at?

   - Students write independently. Encourage at least 5 sentences.
   - Teacher circulates, reading over shoulders. Offer specific praise: *"I love how you used the word 'challenging' instead of 'hard.' That's a great vocabulary choice."*
   - Gently prompt students who are stuck: *"Tell me out loud what you want to write. Good — now write exactly what you just said."*
   - Remind students to use the Sentence Checklist.

3. **Sharing & Peer Feedback (5 min)**:
   - Pair up. Each student reads their journal entry to their partner.
   - Partner gives one compliment: *"I liked the part where you said ___."*
   - 1–2 volunteers share with the whole group.

**Data Collection**:
- Do NOT formally score this journal entry — it is a growth artifact, not an assessment.
- Informally note: Who wrote 5+ sentences? Who struggled to write 3? Spelling trends? Convention trends?
- Compare informally to Wednesday's writing baseline — any differences in length, structure, or confidence?
- This entry stays in the journal for portfolio purposes.

**Differentiation**:
- **Below level**: Provide sentence starters: "My favorite activity was ___." / "Something new I learned was ___." / "Next week I want to ___." Accept 3 sentences. Allow a combination of writing and drawing.
- **On level**: 5+ sentences as described. Use the Sentence Checklist to self-edit.
- **Above level**: Write a full paragraph (7+ sentences) with a clear beginning, middle, and end. Challenge: *"Can you include a simile? For example, 'Learning new math strategies was like getting a new tool in my toolbox.'"*

#### Activity 1: Self-Portrait + "Who I Am as a Learner" Art (12:00–12:25)

**Overview**: Students create a self-portrait that incorporates words and images about who they are as learners. This becomes the cover page of their summer portfolio.

**Materials**:
- Drawing paper (heavy stock, 9x12)
- Small mirrors (1 per student or shared — for observing their own features)
- Colored pencils
- Oil pastels or crayons
- Pencils for sketching
- Examples of self-portrait art (show 2–3 from diverse artists — Frida Kahlo, Jean-Michel Basquiat, children's examples)

**Instructions**:

1. **Introduction (3 min)**:
   - Show 2–3 self-portrait examples. *"Artists create self-portraits to show the world who they are — not just what they look like, but what's inside them."*
   - *"Today you'll create a self-portrait that shows who YOU are as a learner. Around your face, you'll add words and pictures that represent your strengths, interests, and goals."*

2. **Sketching (7 min)**:
   - Students use mirrors to observe their features.
   - Sketch their face in pencil first (center of the paper, taking up about half the page).
   - Teacher tip: *"Start with an oval for your face. Then add your eyes about halfway down. Nose in the middle, mouth below that. Look in the mirror — what makes YOUR face special?"*

3. **Adding Color & Details (10 min)**:
   - Color in the portrait with colored pencils or oil pastels.
   - Around the portrait, students add:
     - 3 words that describe them as a learner (e.g., "curious," "determined," "creative")
     - 1 picture of something they're good at
     - 1 picture of something they want to learn
   - Background can be decorated with patterns, colors, or symbols.

4. **Display (5 min)**:
   - Write their name at the bottom.
   - These are displayed on the "Who We Are" wall — they stay up all summer.
   - **Portfolio piece**: Teacher photographs each self-portrait. The original goes on the wall; a printed photo goes in the portfolio.

**Academic Connection**:
- **Writing**: Vocabulary — choosing precise descriptive words. Adjectives that describe character traits.
- **Reading**: Connecting to read-aloud themes about self-identity and growth mindset.
- **SEL**: Self-awareness, positive self-identity, recognizing strengths.

#### Activity 3: IEP & Thrive Store — First Opening (12:35–12:50)

**Setup**: One corner of the room is the "store." Items are displayed with price tags.

**Store Items & Prices**:
- Extra free time (10 min) — $10
- Choose your seat for a day — $15
- Homework pass — $20
- Small prize from treasure box — $10
- Be the teacher's helper for a block — $15
- Lunch with the teacher — $25
- Draw on the whiteboard for 5 min — $5
- Wear a hat in class for a day — $10

**Process**:
- Students count their wallets (earned throughout the week).
- They may buy one item or save for something more expensive.
- Teacher or assistant acts as cashier. Students must:

  4. Record the transaction in their wallet (write the new balance on the envelope).

**Math Connection**: Real-world application of addition, subtraction, and money skills from this week.
