### Math Block (9:20–10:20)

**Objective**: Complete weekly math progress monitoring probe (fractions + computation). Then engage in a review game covering all Week 5 math concepts. (IEP Goal: Math Computation & Concepts — demonstrate growth on weekly probe.)

**Materials**:
- Weekly Math Probe #4 (prepared — mirrors format from Weeks 1–4):
  - Section A: Fraction comparison (6 items — circle the larger fraction or identify benchmark)
  - Section B: Fraction addition/subtraction with like denominators (4 items — 4th grade) OR fraction equivalence (4 items — 3rd grade)
  - Section C: Money word problems (3 items)
  - Section D: Time problems (2 items — read clock, elapsed time)
  - Section E: Measurement (2 items — read a ruler)
- "Math Review Relay" game cards (prepared — 20 questions covering Weeks 1–5 content)
- Dry-erase boards + markers
- Fraction strips, play money, clocks (available as tools during review game)
- Timer

#### Weekly Probe — Assessment (20 min)

1. **Probe Administration (15 min)**:
   - *"This is your weekly math check. It shows me what you've learned and what we need to keep practicing. Do your best — it's okay if some problems are hard."*
   - Distribute probes. Students work independently.
   - Manipulatives available at the front table — students may get fraction strips, play money, or clocks if needed (note who uses them).
   - Teacher monitors silently, noting: Who finishes quickly? Who is stuck? Who uses tools?

2. **Collect & Quick Debrief (5 min)**:
   - Collect probes.
   - Quick thumbs: *"How did that feel compared to the first week? Better, same, harder?"*
   - *"The fact that many of you just compared fractions using benchmarks — and 4 weeks ago we hadn't even started fractions — shows incredible growth."*

#### Math Review Relay Game (30 min)

- **Setup**: Divide class into 3 teams. Each team lines up.
- **Gameplay**: Teacher reads a question. First student in each team solves on their board and holds it up. Correct answer = 1 point. Then they move to the back of the line.
- **Questions** (sample — covering Weeks 1–5):
  1. "What is the value of the 7 in 3,752?" (700)
  2. "Round 4,381 to the nearest hundred." (4,400)
  3. "5 x 8 = ?" (40)
  4. "24 / 6 = ?" (4)
  5. "Which is larger: 3/4 or 2/4?" (3/4)
  6. "What is 2/5 + 1/5?" (3/5)
  7. "Is 1/8 closer to 0 or 1/2?" (0)
  8. "You have $5.00 and spend $3.25. How much change?" ($1.75)
  9. "What time is it?" (Show clock — 10:45)
  10. "A movie starts at 2:00 and ends at 4:30. How long is it?" (2 hours 30 minutes)
  11. "7 x 6 = ?" (42)
  12. "What is 5/6 - 2/6?" (3/6 = 1/2)
  13. "Write 3,405 in expanded form." (3,000 + 400 + 5)
  14. "You buy 3 items at $2.00 each. How much?" ($6.00)
  15. "Is 5/8 closer to 1/2 or 1?" (1/2 — it's just a little more)
  16. "A pencil is 7 inches long. A pen is 5 inches. What's the difference?" (2 inches)
  17. "Name a fraction equivalent to 1/2 with a denominator of 8." (4/8)
  18. "36 / 4 = ?" (9)
  19. "You buy milk ($4.25) and bread ($3.50). Total?" ($7.75)
  20. "What fraction of a dollar is a quarter?" (1/4)

- Allow manipulatives for any question. Celebrate correct answers AND good effort.

#### Closure (5 min)
- Announce winning team (small reward — sticker or extra free time).
- *"You've reviewed place value, multiplication, division, fractions, time, money, and measurement — all in one game. That's the power of 5 weeks of learning."*

#### Differentiation
- **Below level (Group A)**: During the probe, provide fraction strip and coin value reference cards. During the relay, pair with a stronger partner — they solve together but weaker student holds up the board and explains.
- **On level (Group B)**: Standard probe and relay participation.
- **Above level (Group C)**: Probe includes extension items. During relay, serve as team captain — help teammates but don't solve for them. Bonus challenge questions for extra points.

#### Part 1: Assessment — ORF + Morphology (pulled individually throughout the block)

**While students work on essay finalization and connected reading, teacher pulls students one at a time.**

**ORF Probe — Week 5 (2 min per student)**:
- Use grade-level passage (same difficulty as Weeks 1–4).
- Student reads for 1 minute. Record: Words Correct Per Minute (WCPM) and accuracy %.
- Note prosody observations (phrasing, expression, pace).
- Compare to Week 1 baseline and Weeks 2–4 scores.

**Morphology Assessment (3 min per student)**:
- 20-item assessment:
  - Items 1–5: Identify the prefix and state its meaning (e.g., "unhappy" → un- = not)
  - Items 6–10: Identify the suffix and state its meaning (e.g., "hopeful" → -ful = full of)
  - Items 11–15: Read a word with prefix/suffix and use it correctly in a sentence
  - Items 16–20: Given a base word + affix, spell the new word correctly (applying spelling rules)
    - happy + -ness = happiness (change-y)
    - hope + -ful = hopeful (no change — consonant suffix)
    - kind + -ly = kindly (no change)
    - sad + -ness = sadness (no doubling needed — ends in 2 consonants... wait, just d — but "sad" → "sadness" no double because -ness starts with consonant)
    - un- + do = undo

- Score: ___/20. Record by category (prefix ID, suffix ID, usage, spelling).

#### Part 2: Opinion Essay Finalization (whole group — 20 min, 10:30–10:50)

**Objective**: Students will complete and finalize their "Why ___ Matters" opinion essay for their portfolio.

**Materials**:
- Students' revised drafts (from Thursday)
- Clean lined paper (for final copy)
- Editing checklist (CUPS)
- Colored pencils
- Portfolio folders

**Lesson**:

1. **Final Copy Instructions (3 min)**:
   - *"Today you write the FINAL version of your 'Why ___ Matters' essay. This goes in your portfolio — it's one of the pieces your family will see at the showcase."*
   - Checklist on the board:
     - [ ] My opinion is stated clearly in the first sentence.
     - [ ] I have at least 2 reasons with transition words.
     - [ ] I have a closing sentence that restates my opinion.
     - [ ] I checked CUPS: Capitalization, Understanding, Punctuation, Spelling.
     - [ ] I wrote neatly and clearly.
     - [ ] My name and the date are at the top.

2. **Writing Time (15 min)**:
   - Students write their final copies.
   - Teacher pulls students for ORF/morphology assessments during this time.
   - Teaching assistant or volunteer monitors the writing group.
   - Students who finish early: read their essay to a partner for a final listen-check. Then begin a cover page illustration for their essay.

3. **Collection (2 min)**:
   - Collect all final essays. Place in portfolio folders.
   - *"I'll score these using our opinion writing rubric and include them in your final report."*

**Writing Rubric** (shared with students — posted):
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 4 | Clear opinion, 3+ strong reasons with examples, transition words, strong closing, few/no errors |
| 3 | Clear opinion, 2 reasons with transitions, closing, minor errors |
| 2 | Opinion stated, 1–2 reasons (may lack transitions or closing), some errors |
| 1 | Opinion unclear or missing, reasons weak or absent, significant errors |

#### Differentiation
- **Below level**: Teacher or aide helps finalize during conference time. Accept a shorter essay (4–5 sentences) with clear opinion + 2 reasons + closing. May use the writing frame template as the final copy format.
- **On level**: Write final copy independently. Score target: 3/4 on rubric.
- **Above level**: Final copy includes counterargument. Write independently with strong word choice and varied sentence structure. Score target: 4/4.

#### Part 3: Connected Reading — Morphology in Context (10:50–11:30)

**While assessments continue, remaining students engage in connected reading.**

**Activity: "Morpheme Detective" Independent Reading** (20 min):
- Students read from their choice book or a provided text at their level.
- Task: Find and record 10 words with prefixes or suffixes in their reading journal.
- For each word, record:
  - The word
  - The base word
  - The prefix or suffix
  - The meaning of the whole word
- Format example: *"unhappy → happy + un- (not) → not happy"*

**Word Web Extension** (10 min):
- Students choose their best base word from the detective activity.
- Create a word web with as many derived forms as possible.
- Share with a partner: *"I found the base word 'care.' From it, I built: careful, careless, carefully, carelessly, uncaring, carefree..."*

**Morphology Board Game** (if time allows):
- Pairs play a morpheme matching game: flip a prefix/suffix card and a base word card. If they make a real word, keep the pair. Most pairs wins.

#### Differentiation
- **Below level**: Provide a prepared text with 10 target words highlighted. Students identify the affix for each highlighted word using a reference card.
- **On level**: Find 10 words independently. Complete the journal recording format.
- **Above level**: Find 15 words. Categorize by prefix vs. suffix. Identify any words with BOTH a prefix and a suffix. Challenge: find a word with a prefix or suffix that you haven't learned yet, and try to figure out its meaning from context.

### Exploration & Enrichment (12:00–1:00)

**Activity**: Persuasive Poster Creation & Presentation

**Overview**: Students create a persuasive poster about a real-world issue they care about, applying opinion writing skills (claim + reasons) in a visual format. They present their posters to the class.

**Materials**:
- Poster board or large paper (1 per student)
- Markers, colored pencils, crayons
- Glue sticks, scissors
- Magazines for cutting out images (optional)
- "Persuasive Poster Checklist" (posted):
  - [ ] Bold title/headline that states your opinion
  - [ ] At least 2 reasons supporting your opinion
  - [ ] A picture or image that supports your message
  - [ ] A call to action: What should people DO?
  - [ ] Neat, readable, colorful
- Timer

**Schedule**:

1. **Introduction & Planning (8 min)**:
   - *"Today you're creating a persuasive poster — like a billboard or advertisement that tries to convince people to agree with you or take action."*
   - Show 2–3 example posters (teacher-made or printed):
     - "Save Water — Every Drop Counts!" with reasons and a picture
     - "Read Every Day!" with benefits listed
   - Review the checklist.
   - Students plan on a scrap paper: What's their topic? What's the headline? What are the reasons? What image?
   - Possible topics: recycling, saving water, reading, exercise, community gardens, kindness, helping others, protecting animals, reducing screen time. Students may also choose their opinion essay topic.

2. **Poster Creation (25 min)**:
   - Students create their posters.
   - Teacher circulates with feedback: *"Is your headline clear? Can I read it from across the room? Do you have reasons, not just your opinion?"*
   - Encourage: large letters, bold colors, organized layout.
   - 5-minute warning to finish up.

3. **Presentations (15 min)**:
   - Each student presents their poster (1–2 min each):
     - State the opinion.
     - Share the reasons.
     - Read the call to action.
   - Audience practices active listening. After each presentation: one compliment from a classmate.
   - *"You just practiced the same skills you'll use at next week's showcase — presenting your work and your ideas to an audience!"*

4. **Gallery Display (2 min)**:
   - Hang posters on the wall. These stay up for the showcase next week.

5. **Closing Circle — Week 5 Wrap-Up (10 min)**:
   - *"It's the end of Week 5 — Real-World Problem Solvers. Let's go around the circle: What is one real-world skill you practiced this week?"*
   - Review the week: garden measurement, community helpers, design challenge, grocery budgeting, persuasive posters.
   - *"You've been solving problems all week — as readers, writers, mathematicians, scientists, and citizens."*
   - Self-monitoring checklist — final check for the week.
   - Remind about home practice: read 10 min + practice red words + 5 min real-world math.
   - **IMPORTANT**: *"Next week is our LAST WEEK. Monday through Thursday we review everything, finish our portfolios, and take our final assessments. Friday is the FAMILY SHOWCASE at 11:30 AM. Make sure your families know!"*
   - Dismissal at 1:00.

**Academic Connections**:
- **Writing**: Persuasive writing in a visual format — claim, reasons, call to action.
- **Reading**: Author's purpose (they ARE the author now — persuading!).
- **Math**: Some posters may include data or statistics.
- **Art**: Visual design, layout, color choice for communication.

**Differentiation**:
- **Below level**: Provide a poster template with sections labeled (Title Here, Reason 1 Here, Reason 2 Here, Picture Here, Call to Action Here). Teacher helps brainstorm headline. Accept simpler text. Presentation: may read directly from poster with teacher prompting.
- **On level**: Full activity. Create independently with poster checklist. Present with 2+ reasons.
- **Above level**: Include a statistic or data point on the poster. Add a counterargument section: "Some people say ___, but..." Present with eye contact and respond to one audience question.
