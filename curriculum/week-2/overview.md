# Week 2 — "Patterns & Connections"
## Theme: Patterns in Language and Numbers

**Dates**: July 14–18, 2026
**Focus**: Vowel team patterns (Part 1), paragraph structure, multiplication concepts, pattern recognition across domains

---

## Weekly Learning Objectives

### Reading & Phonics (OG)
- Introduce vowel team syllable type with multisensory drill (sky writing, sand trays, phonogram cards)
- Teach vowel teams: ai/ay (rain, play), ee/ea (feet, beach), oa/ow (boat, snow)
- Distinguish between two spellings of the same sound (e.g., ai in the middle vs. ay at the end of a word/syllable)
- Practice decoding and encoding words with target vowel teams in isolation and connected text
- OG lesson structure each day: review drill (previous patterns) → new teach → word list practice → dictation → decodable text
- Red words for the week: could, would, should, again, because, people, friend
- Continue individualized red word review from Week 1 set for students who need it

### Comprehension
- Introduce main idea vs. topic distinction ("What is this mostly about?" vs. "What is the one big idea the author wants you to know?")
- Teach identifying supporting details using a graphic organizer (main idea web)
- Model finding the main idea in short informational paragraphs (3–5 sentences)
- Practice with both teacher read-alouds and student-read passages at instructional level
- Introduce "Somebody Wanted But So Then" (SWBST) for summarizing narrative text

### Writing
- Teach paragraph structure explicitly: topic sentence, 2–3 detail sentences, closing sentence
- Use the "hamburger paragraph" model (top bun = topic sentence, fillings = details, bottom bun = closing)
- Follow the modeled → shared → guided → independent progression across the week
- Monday: teacher models a paragraph about a pattern in nature
- Tuesday: shared writing — class co-constructs a paragraph
- Wednesday: guided writing with sentence starters and graphic organizer
- Thursday: independent paragraph writing (enrichment connection — "A Pattern I Discovered")
- Friday: peer sharing and self-assessment with paragraph checklist

### Math
- Introduce multiplication as equal groups using concrete manipulatives (counters, snap cubes)
- Build and interpret arrays (rows x columns) using tiles and grid paper
- Connect skip counting to multiplication (number line hops)
- Teach foundational fact strategies: x0 (zero property), x1 (identity property), x2 (doubles), x5 (clock connection), x10 (place value connection)
- CRA progression: Monday–Tuesday concrete (manipulatives), Wednesday representational (array drawings, equal group diagrams), Thursday–Friday abstract (equations and fact practice)
- Word problems with equal groups schema: "___ groups of ___ = ___"

### Executive Function & SEL
- Introduce "pattern thinking" as a learning strategy: looking for what repeats, what is predictable
- Practice task initiation strategies: "Where do I start? What do I do first?"
- Continue self-monitoring checklist use with increased independence
- SEL focus: recognizing patterns in our emotions — "When I feel ___, I can ___"
- Collaborative learning: partner work routines for math array building

## Differentiation Notes

### Flexible Grouping (Based on Week 1 Assessment Data)

**Phonics Groups** (for OG vowel team instruction):
- Group A: Students still solidifying blends/digraphs — continue CVC+ practice; introduce only ai/ay vowel team with heavy scaffolding; additional time with decodable texts at blend/digraph level
- Group B: Blends/digraphs mastered, ready for vowel teams — teach all three pairs (ai/ay, ee/ea, oa/ow) at standard pace; use the full OG lesson cycle daily
- Group C: Some vowel team exposure from prior instruction — accelerated pace with all three pairs; add encoding (spelling) emphasis and connected text fluency practice; challenge with vowel team words in multi-syllable context (e.g., explain, repeat)

**Math Groups**:
- Group A: Still building addition/subtraction fluency — introduce multiplication conceptually through equal groups only; use smaller factors (x1, x2, x5); additional fact fluency practice with addition/subtraction built into warm-ups
- Group B: Addition/subtraction fluent, ready for multiplication — full progression through equal groups, arrays, and beginning fact strategies; standard word problem practice
- Group C: Some multiplication exposure — move more quickly to representational/abstract; introduce x3 and x4 in addition to core facts; challenge with two-step word problems involving addition and multiplication

**Comprehension**: All students work with the same strategies (main idea, supporting details) but with texts at their instructional reading level. Teacher-read passages used for whole group; leveled passages for guided/independent practice.

**Writing**: All students use the hamburger paragraph model. Scaffold with:
- Sentence starters for students who need them
- Word banks posted and in folders
- Graphic organizer required for all; some students may move to drafting without it by Friday
